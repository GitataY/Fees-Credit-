# Feature: Zeraki Fee Top-Up Credit

## Overview
**What it does**: Identifies parents with a strong fee payment track record, delivers a "fee top-up" offer via the Zeraki app or SMS, collects two-step explicit consent, coordinates disbursement directly to the school's paybill via a lending partner, and recovers repayment automatically through a waterfall allocation built into the parent's existing fee payment flow.
**Why it exists**: Parents who cannot cover the full term fee today borrow from Tala or shylocks at 15–20% monthly interest. This product offers the same bridge at ~5% flat — purpose-locked to school fees, requiring no new parent behaviour to repay. Zeraki's multi-term payment history data is the underwriting input that no standalone lender can replicate.
**Phase**: Phase 1 (Path 1 — historically strong payers). Path 2 (Layer 1 plan-adherent parents) is Phase 1 but activates after at least one term of Layer 1 data exists.
**Depends on**:
- Existing Zeraki Finance multi-term fee payment history (Path 1 — technically independent of Layer 1)
- `1.0.1-zeraki-instalment-plans.md` plan adherence records (Path 2 — on-time rate per plan)
- Validated lending partner integration (Pezesha is the candidate — not yet confirmed; see OQ-01)
- School's M-Pesa paybill integration (already exists in Zeraki Finance)
- Layer 2 school opt-in (separate from Layer 1 opt-in)
**This is NOT**:
- A general-purpose lending product — credit is purpose-locked to school fee gaps only
- A product Zeraki capitalises — Zeraki holds no loan book, provides no capital, and takes no default risk
- An automatic Layer 1 upsell — Layer 2 requires its own explicit school opt-in and separate parent consent
- A collections business — Zeraki's system routes waterfall payments passively; no active debt recovery function exists within this feature
- Available in geographic markets where the M-Pesa paybill waterfall model does not port without new infrastructure (see OQ-02)

## Credit Scoring Model

The credit score determines eligibility and the maximum credit amount offered. It is computed during the eligibility assessment batch (Flow 2) for every parent who clears at least one eligibility path's entry requirement.

### Scoring Components

| Component | Weight | What it measures | How it is calculated |
|---|---|---|---|
| Payment speed | 30% | How quickly the parent pays each term | Average % of term fee paid within the first 4 weeks, across the last 4 terms. Score = average × 100 (0–100). |
| Payment consistency | 25% | How regular and predictable the payment pattern is across terms | Inverse of the coefficient of variation (CV) of the weekly-4 payment percentage across the last 4 terms. Score = max(0, 100 − (CV × 100)). A parent who always pays ~90% in week 4 scores near 100; a parent who pays 10% one term and 95% the next scores low. |
| Pledge / plan fulfilment rate | 20% | Whether the parent keeps payment commitments | If Layer 1 data exists: the parent's `completion_rate` from their most recently completed plan × 100. If no Layer 1 data: the historical pledge fulfilment rate from Zeraki Finance (% of recorded pledges honoured) × 100. If neither exists: score = 50 (neutral). |
| Tenure | 15% | Depth of payment history in the system | Step function: 1 term = 20, 2 terms = 40, 3 terms = 60, 4 terms = 75, 5 terms = 85, 6+ terms = 100. |
| Current term effort | 10% | How much of the current term's fee the parent has already paid | % of current term fee already paid at time of assessment × 100. |

### Weighted Score Calculation

```
credit_score = (payment_speed × 0.30)
             + (payment_consistency × 0.25)
             + (plan_fulfilment × 0.20)
             + (tenure × 0.15)
             + (current_term_effort × 0.10)
```

Score range: 0–100.

### Credit Tiers

| Tier | Score range | Maximum credit amount |
|---|---|---|
| A | 80–100 | Lesser of 30% of the student's term fee or KES 10,000 |
| B | 60–79 | Lesser of 20% of the student's term fee or KES 6,000 |
| C | 40–59 | Lesser of 15% of the student's term fee or KES 3,000 |
| Not eligible | Below 40 | No offer |

**Minimum offer floor**: If the calculated maximum credit amount is less than KES 3,000, no offer is made regardless of tier.

**Lending partner override**: The lending partner may approve a lower credit amount than the tier maximum. In that case, the offer to the parent reflects the lending partner's approved amount, not Zeraki's tier maximum.

## Actors

- **Headteacher / School Admin**: Opts the school into Fee Top-Up Credit via a separate settings toggle. Agrees to the school's role in the repayment waterfall (their paybill is used as the repayment routing channel).
- **Bursar**: Views credit arrangement status and total disbursed to school this term in the dashboard. Takes no active role in the credit or repayment process.
- **Parent**: Receives and responds to the credit offer. Provides two-step explicit consent. Repays by continuing to pay fees to the school's paybill as normal — no new repayment behaviour required.
- **Lending Partner**: Provides credit capital and holds the lending licence. Bears all default risk. Receives pre-qualified borrower data and disbursement requests from Zeraki. Disburses directly to school's paybill. Receives repayment shares via B2B transfer from Zeraki's waterfall. (Pezesha is the candidate partner — not yet validated against actual API or commercial terms.)

---

## Preconditions

- The school has an active Zeraki Finance subscription with Fee Top-Up Credit enabled (Flow 1)
- A validated lending partner integration exists and is live (OQ-01 — currently unvalidated)
- The student has an outstanding fee balance for the current term
- The parent has a phone number registered against the student record in Zeraki Finance
- The parent meets at least one eligibility path's criteria (assessed by the system in Flow 2)
- The parent has not already accepted a credit arrangement for this student this term
- The school's M-Pesa paybill integration is active in Zeraki Finance

## Core Flows

### Flow 1: School Enables Fee Top-Up Credit

**Trigger**: Headteacher or designated admin navigates to Settings → Fee Collection → Fee Top-Up Credit.

**Steps**:
1. Admin opens the Fee Top-Up Credit settings screen.
2. System displays a summary of how the product works: eligible parents receive an offer, credit is disbursed directly to the school's paybill by a lending partner, repayment is automatically routed through future fee payments.
3. System displays the school commitment: *"By enabling Fee Top-Up Credit, this school agrees that its M-Pesa paybill will be used as the repayment routing channel — incoming fee payments from parents with active credit arrangements will be automatically split between the school's fee balance and the lending partner's repayment share."*
4. Admin confirms by clicking **Enable Fee Top-Up Credit**.
5. System activates the feature for the school.
6. System confirms: *"Fee Top-Up Credit is now enabled. Eligible parents will be identified at the start of each term and offered fee support."*

**Outcome**: The school is opted in. Eligibility assessment will run for this school in the next scheduled batch job.

**Error — Layer 1 not enabled**:
- System displays: *"Please enable Instalment Plans first. Fee Top-Up Credit requires instalment plan data to assess eligibility for some parents."*
- Fee Top-Up Credit cannot be enabled without Layer 1 being active. (Note: Path 1 eligibility is technically independent, but the product is deployed together.)

---

### Flow 2: System Runs Eligibility Assessment

**Trigger**: Scheduled batch job runs at the start of each term (and optionally mid-term — see OQ-03) for all schools with Fee Top-Up Credit enabled.

**Steps**:
1. System identifies all students with an outstanding fee balance for the current term at opted-in schools.
2. For each student, system assesses the parent against both eligibility paths:

   **Path 1 — Historically Strong Payer**:
   - System retrieves the parent's fee payment records for the student across all available terms in Zeraki Finance.
   - Requires minimum 4 terms of fee history.
   - Calculates `path_1_score`: percentage of terms where the full fee was paid by end of term (see BR-03 for exact calculation).
   - Parent is Path 1 eligible if `path_1_score ≥ [threshold]` (see OQ-04).

   **Path 2 — Layer 1 Plan-Adherent**:
   - System retrieves all completed or active instalment plans for this student in Zeraki Finance.
   - Parent is Path 2 eligible if the most recent completed plan has `on_time_rate ≥ 0.75` — even if the current term's plan shows a disruption (deviation vs. pattern logic).
   - Requires at least one completed Layer 1 plan.

3. For each parent who meets at least one path's entry requirement, system computes the weighted credit score (see Credit Scoring Model):
   - Retrieves all five scoring component inputs from Zeraki Finance data
   - Calculates each component score (0–100)
   - Applies the weighted formula to produce the final `credit_score`
   - Maps the score to a credit tier (A / B / C / Not eligible)
   - Calculates the maximum credit offer amount based on the tier formula and the student's term fee
4. System excludes any parent whose calculated offer amount is below KES 3,000 (minimum floor).
5. System creates an eligibility record for each eligible parent, including the credit score, tier, and maximum offer amount (see Data Contracts).
6. System sends the eligibility record to the lending partner for pre-approval review (see Lending Partner Disbursement Request). The lending partner may approve a lower amount than Zeraki's tier maximum — the approved amount is stored on the eligibility record.
7. Eligible parents with an active plan request or an existing credit arrangement this term are excluded (no double-offer).

**Outcome**: Eligibility records exist for all qualifying parents. These records feed Flow 3 (offer delivery).

**Edge — Parent qualifies under both Path 1 and Path 2**:
- System uses Path 1 as the primary eligibility signal. Path 2 is noted on the record but Path 1 takes precedence for underwriting purposes (stronger signal). The maximum credit amount is calculated the same way regardless of path.

**Edge — Parent has less than 6 terms of data and no completed Layer 1 plan**:
- Parent is not eligible. No eligibility record is created. No offer is sent.

---

### Flow 3: Credit Offer Delivered to Parent

**Trigger**: Eligibility assessment batch completes. System delivers offers to all newly eligible parents who have not yet been offered or accepted credit this term.

**Steps**:
1. System sends an offer notification to each eligible parent via the school's sender ID:
   - **App**: push notification and in-app banner on the student's fee screen
   - **SMS**: offer SMS (see SMS Contracts — Credit Offer)
2. System creates a credit arrangement record in **OFFERED** status.
3. System records the offer timestamp. Offer expires after [N] days if not accepted (see OQ-05).

**Outcome**: Parent receives offer. Credit arrangement record exists in OFFERED state. Offer expiry timer starts.

**Note on language**: The offer never uses the word "loan". Entry language uses "fee support" or "fee top-up". The credit nature is disclosed fully in the consent step (Flow 4 / Flow 5) — this is progressive disclosure, not concealment.

---

### Flow 4: Parent Accepts Offer via App (Two-Step Consent)

**Trigger**: Parent opens the offer notification in the Zeraki app.

**Steps**:
1. Parent opens the offer screen. System displays:
   - The credit amount available (e.g., *"KES 4,500 fee support available for [Student name]"*)
   - How it works: *"This amount will be paid directly to [School name] on your behalf. You repay through your regular fee payments — a portion of each payment you make this term and next term will go towards repaying the support."*
   - The repayment schedule: estimated weekly or monthly repayment amounts based on the credit amount
2. **Consent Step 1**: Parent is shown the consent disclosure:
   - *"To receive this offer, [School name] will share your fee payment history with our credit partner."*
   - *"Repayment activity will be reported to credit reference bureaus."*
   - Parent taps **I understand — continue**.
3. **Consent Step 2**: System shows a final confirmation:
   - *"Confirm fee top-up of KES [amount] for [Student name]. Repayment of approximately KES [amount] per [week/month] starts from your next fee payment. Do you confirm?"*
   - Parent taps **Confirm**.
4. System records consent (timestamp, channel: `app`, consent version).
5. System transitions the credit arrangement to **ACCEPTED** status.
6. System triggers Flow 7 (disbursement coordination with lending partner).
7. System sends acceptance confirmation SMS to parent (see SMS Contracts — Offer Accepted).

**Outcome**: Parent has given full, informed, two-step consent. Credit arrangement is ACCEPTED. Disbursement process begins.

**Error — Parent taps back or dismisses at any consent step**:
- Arrangement remains in OFFERED status. No consent is recorded. Parent can return to the offer later (until expiry).

---

### Flow 5: Parent Accepts Offer via SMS (Two-Step Consent)

**Trigger**: Parent receives the credit offer SMS and replies.

**Steps**:
1. Parent replies **FEES** to the school's inbound SMS number.
2. System sends Step 1 disclosure SMS (see SMS Contracts — Consent Step 1).
3. Parent replies **YES**.
4. System sends Step 2 confirmation SMS (see SMS Contracts — Consent Step 2).
5. Parent replies **CONFIRM**.
6. System records consent (timestamp, channel: `sms`, consent version).
7. System transitions the credit arrangement to **ACCEPTED** status.
8. System triggers Flow 7 (disbursement coordination).
9. System sends acceptance confirmation SMS (see SMS Contracts — Offer Accepted).

**Outcome**: Identical to Flow 4. Full two-step consent recorded via SMS.

**Error — Parent replies something other than YES at Step 3**:
- System replies: *"Reply YES to continue or ignore this message to decline."*
- SMS session remains open for 30 minutes. After 30 minutes, session expires. Arrangement remains OFFERED.

**Error — Parent replies something other than CONFIRM at Step 5**:
- System replies: *"Reply CONFIRM to complete your fee top-up, or ignore to cancel."*
- Same 30-minute window applies.

**Error — Parent does not respond within session window**:
- Session expires. Arrangement remains OFFERED until the offer expiry date. Parent can re-initiate by sending the keyword again.

---

### Flow 6: Parent Declines Offer or Offer Expires

**Trigger A**: Parent taps **No thanks** in the app, or replies **NO** or **STOP** to the offer SMS.
**Trigger B**: Offer expiry date is reached with no response.

**Steps (Decline)**:
1. System transitions the credit arrangement to **DECLINED**.
2. No SMS is sent on decline — the parent actively chose to decline and no confirmation is necessary.
3. System records the decline timestamp and channel.

**Steps (Expiry)**:
1. System transitions the credit arrangement to **EXPIRED**.
2. System sends an expiry notification SMS (see SMS Contracts — Offer Expired).

**Outcome**: No credit arrangement is created. The parent's eligibility record is preserved for future term assessments.

**Note**: A declined or expired parent is not re-offered in the same term. They may be assessed again and offered at the start of the next term if still eligible.

---

### Flow 7: Zeraki Coordinates Disbursement with Lending Partner

**Trigger**: Credit arrangement transitions to ACCEPTED (from Flow 4 or Flow 5).

**Steps**:
1. Zeraki system sends a disbursement request to the lending partner's API (see Data Contracts — Lending Partner Disbursement Request).
   - Includes: arrangement ID, credit amount, school paybill number, student reference, consent timestamp and version, eligibility path and score.
2. Lending partner validates the request and initiates disbursement to the school's M-Pesa paybill.
3. Lending partner sends a disbursement confirmation to Zeraki's webhook (see Data Contracts — Lending Partner Disbursement Confirmation).
4. System transitions the credit arrangement to **DISBURSED** then immediately to **ACTIVE_REPAYMENT**.
5. System records: disbursement amount, lending partner reference, disbursement timestamp.
6. System notifies the bursar via the dashboard: one new disbursement to school credited this term (no SMS to bursar — dashboard update only).
7. System sends a disbursement confirmation SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Credit Disbursed).

**Outcome**: Credit has been paid to the school's paybill. School is closer to whole. Repayment waterfall is now live for this arrangement.

**Error — Lending partner rejects the disbursement request**:
- System transitions the arrangement to **DISBURSEMENT_FAILED**.
- System records the rejection reason from the lending partner response.
- System sends an SMS to the parent: *"We were unable to process your fee top-up at this time. Please contact [School name] for assistance."*
- No retry is automatic — requires manual investigation (see OQ-06).

**Error — Lending partner API is unreachable (timeout)**:
- System queues the request for retry after [N] minutes (see OQ-07).
- After [M] retries with no response, arrangement is flagged for manual review.
- Parent is not notified until disbursement is confirmed.

---

### Flow 8: Waterfall Repayment Allocation

**Trigger**: A fee payment is received on the school's M-Pesa paybill for a student whose parent has an **ACTIVE_REPAYMENT** credit arrangement.

**Steps**:
1. Payment event arrives via the Zeraki Finance paybill webhook (same webhook as Layer 1 payment matching).
2. System checks whether the paying student has an ACTIVE_REPAYMENT arrangement.
3. System calculates the repayment share for this payment (see BR-07 for waterfall priority rules).
4. System splits the payment:
   - **Repayment share** → queued for B2B transfer to the lending partner (amount ≤ scheduled repayment instalment for this period)
   - **Remainder** → credited to the school's fee balance for this student as normal
5. System records the waterfall allocation event (see Data Contracts — Waterfall Allocation Event).
6. System updates the credit arrangement: remaining balance reduced by repayment share.
7. System sends a payment allocation SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Waterfall Payment Applied).
8. If remaining credit balance = 0: system transitions arrangement to **COMPLETED** and triggers Flow 9.
9. B2B transfer to lending partner is executed (batched per OQ-08 — daily or per-transaction).

**Outcome**: Parent's fee payment is split. School receives its share. Lending partner repayment share is queued for transfer. Parent is notified. No new behaviour is required from the parent.

**Edge — Payment amount is less than the scheduled repayment instalment amount**:
- System takes the entire payment as the repayment share first, then credits zero to the school's fee balance.
- System sends SMS to parent: *"KES [amount] received for [Student]. Full amount applied to your fee top-up balance. Remaining top-up: KES [amount]. School balance unchanged this payment."* (See OQ-09 for whether this priority order is correct — confirm with lending partner.)

**Edge — Payment amount exceeds the remaining credit balance**:
- System takes only the remaining credit balance as the repayment share.
- Surplus is credited to the school's fee balance.
- Arrangement transitions to COMPLETED.

**Edge — Parent has both an active Layer 1 instalment plan AND an active credit arrangement**:
- The waterfall allocation for credit repayment takes priority over instalment plan milestone tracking (see BR-08).
- After the repayment share is allocated, the remaining payment is applied to the Layer 1 plan per the FIFO rules in `1.0.1-zeraki-instalment-plans.md`.

---

### Flow 9: Credit Arrangement Completed

**Trigger**: Waterfall allocation reduces the remaining credit balance to zero (from Flow 8).

**Steps**:
1. System transitions the arrangement to **COMPLETED**.
2. System records the completion timestamp.
3. System sends completion SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Credit Completed).
4. System sends completion data to the lending partner (arrangement closed, final repayment reference).
5. Bursar dashboard updates: arrangement moves from active to completed count.

**Outcome**: Credit arrangement is fully repaid. Parent's payment history is updated. Arrangement is closed.

---

### Flow 10: Waterfall Breaks — Child No Longer Enrolled

**Trigger**: System detects that a student with an ACTIVE_REPAYMENT arrangement has been withdrawn or transferred — either through a withdrawal event in Zeraki Finance or through [N] consecutive terms with no fee payment activity (see OQ-10).

**Steps**:
1. System transitions the arrangement to **AT_RISK** status and flags it for review.
2. System notifies the lending partner via API: arrangement is at risk, waterfall collection has stopped.
3. System sends a bridging SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — AT_RISK notification): *"[School]: Your fee top-up balance for [Student] remains outstanding. The credit partner may contact you about the remaining balance."*
4. Zeraki does not chase the parent — the lending partner holds the credit risk and determines the recovery path.
5. Lending partner may initiate CRB listing for the outstanding balance (per the consent terms the parent agreed to at acceptance).
6. Zeraki records the at-risk event but takes no further action within this system.

**Outcome**: Zeraki has fulfilled its obligation by notifying the lending partner and informing the parent. Default recovery is outside Zeraki's scope.

**Note**: This is the primary residual risk of the waterfall model. It is mitigated by: (a) small credit amounts (KES 3,000–10,000), (b) strict eligibility criteria reducing the likelihood of sudden withdrawal, and (c) CRB listing as a deterrent. Zeraki's commercial exposure is zero — the lending partner bears the loss.

---

### Flow 11: Bursar Views the Credit Dashboard

**Trigger**: Bursar navigates to the Fee Top-Up Credit section of Zeraki Finance.

**Steps**:
1. Bursar opens the Fee Top-Up Credit dashboard.
2. System displays the term-level summary:
   - **Total disbursed to school this term**: total KES credited to the school's paybill via credit arrangements
   - **Active arrangements**: count of ACTIVE_REPAYMENT arrangements
   - **Completed arrangements**: count of COMPLETED arrangements this term
   - **At-risk arrangements**: count of AT_RISK arrangements (if any)
   - Per-student list: student name, credit amount, disbursed date, repayment collected to date, remaining balance, arrangement status
3. Bursar can view an individual arrangement's detail: timeline of disbursement, waterfall allocations, current balance.
4. Bursar cannot modify, approve, or decline credit arrangements — this dashboard is read-only.

**Outcome**: Bursar has visibility into the financial impact of credit disbursements on school cash flow and the current repayment status of all arrangements.

## SMS Contracts

All SMS sent from the school's registered sender ID. Maximum 160 characters per part.

| Event | Recipient | Message |
|---|---|---|
| **Credit offer** | Parent | `[School]: Based on [Student]'s payment record, you may qualify for fee support this term. Reply FEES to see your offer, or ignore to skip.` |
| **Consent Step 1** | Parent | `[School] fee top-up: [Student]'s payment history will be shared with our credit partner. Repayments reported to credit bureaus. Reply YES to continue or NO to cancel.` |
| **Consent Step 2** | Parent | `Confirm: KES [amount] fee top-up for [Student]. ~KES [amount]/[week or month] from your future fee payments. Reply CONFIRM to accept.` |
| **Offer accepted / credit disbursed** | Parent | `[School]: KES [amount] fee top-up confirmed for [Student] and credited to the school. Repayment starts from your next fee payment. Pay to [paybill] as usual.` |
| **Offer declined** | — | No SMS sent on active decline |
| **Offer expired** | Parent | `[School]: Your fee top-up offer for [Student] has expired. Contact the school if you still need support.` |
| **Waterfall payment applied** | Parent | `[School]: KES [amount] received for [Student]. KES [repayment] applied to fee top-up. Remaining: KES [balance]. School fees: KES [fee_credit] credited.` |
| **Credit completed** | Parent | `[School]: Your fee top-up for [Student] is fully repaid. Thank you for keeping your commitment.` |
| **Disbursement failed** | Parent | `[School]: We could not process your fee top-up at this time. Please contact the school for assistance.` |
| **AT_RISK notification** | Parent | `[School]: Your fee top-up balance for [Student] remains outstanding. The credit partner may contact you about the remaining balance.` |

## State Machine

### Credit Arrangement Status
```
[OFFERED]
  --parent accepts (app or SMS, two-step consent)--> [ACCEPTED]
  --parent declines--> [DECLINED]
  --expiry date reached with no response--> [EXPIRED]

[ACCEPTED]
  --disbursement request sent to lending partner--> [DISBURSED]
  --lending partner rejects--> [DISBURSEMENT_FAILED]

[DISBURSED]
  --disbursement confirmed, waterfall active--> [ACTIVE_REPAYMENT]

[ACTIVE_REPAYMENT]
  --remaining balance reaches zero--> [COMPLETED]
  --child withdrawn or transferred--> [AT_RISK]

[AT_RISK]
  --lending partner resolves (repaid or written off)--> [COMPLETED | DEFAULTED]
```

### Repayment Instalment Status
```
[PENDING] --waterfall allocation covers instalment--> [COLLECTED]
[PENDING] --due date passes, no allocation received--> [MISSED]
```

## Business Rules

- **BR-01**: A parent may have at most one active credit arrangement per student per term. Attempting to create a second arrangement returns `ARRANGEMENT_ALREADY_EXISTS`.
- **BR-02**: Credit is offered only to parents who meet at least one eligibility path's criteria. The system does not offer credit to parents who fail both paths.
- **BR-03 — Path 1 eligibility definition**: A parent is Path 1 eligible if their average payment speed score across the last 4 terms is ≥ 90. Payment speed score per term = percentage of the term's total fee that was paid within the first 4 weeks of that term (expressed as 0–100). Minimum 4 terms of fee history required. Example: a parent who paid 95%, 88%, 92%, and 94% of fees in the first 4 weeks across 4 terms has an average of 92.25 — eligible. A parent who paid 60%, 40%, 85%, 95% has an average of 70 — not Path 1 eligible on speed alone, but may qualify via the full credit score (see Credit Scoring Model).
- **BR-04 — Credit amount is tier-based**: The offered credit amount is not a fixed cap. It is determined by the parent's weighted credit score (see Credit Scoring Model). If the calculated offer amount is less than KES 3,000, no offer is made (below minimum). The lending partner may approve a lower amount than Zeraki's tier recommendation — in that case, the lending partner's approved amount is the offer amount.
- **BR-05 — Path 2 eligibility**: Parent is eligible if their most recent completed Layer 1 instalment plan has `on_time_rate ≥ 0.75`. A plan that is currently ACTIVE (in-progress) is not assessed for Path 2 — only completed plans qualify.
- **BR-06 — Deviation vs. pattern (Path 1)**: A parent with 6+ terms of strong payment history (meets BR-03 threshold) is eligible even if the current term's payment activity is poor. The current term is excluded from the path_1_score calculation if the arrangement is being assessed mid-term. This is the "deviation vs. pattern" logic — one bad term does not override a multi-term track record.
- **BR-07 — Waterfall priority**: When a fee payment arrives and a credit arrangement is ACTIVE_REPAYMENT, the repayment share is deducted first. The remainder is credited to the school fee balance. If the payment amount is less than the scheduled repayment instalment, the entire payment goes to the repayment share and zero is credited to the school balance for that payment. (See OQ-09 — confirm priority order with lending partner.)
- **BR-08 — Waterfall + Layer 1 coexistence**: If a parent has both an active credit arrangement (ACTIVE_REPAYMENT) and an active Layer 1 instalment plan, the waterfall repayment share is deducted first. The remaining amount is then applied to the Layer 1 plan per the FIFO rules in `features/1.0.1-zeraki-instalment-plans.md`.
- **BR-09 — Consent version tracking**: The exact text shown to the parent at consent steps 1 and 2 must be versioned. The consent version must be stored on the arrangement record. If the consent disclosure text is ever updated, existing arrangements retain their original consent version. New arrangements use the new version.
- **BR-10 — No raw PII in disbursement request**: The lending partner disbursement request must not include the parent's name or phone number. A hashed borrower reference is used. Full PII sharing terms are governed by the data sharing agreement and the consent disclosure — not by the disbursement API call.
- **BR-11 — Zeraki fee collection timing**: Zeraki's data and distribution fee (1–2% of disbursed amount) is collected at disbursement confirmation. The exact fee rate and collection mechanism are defined in the lending partner commercial agreement (see OQ-12).
- **BR-12 — No parent-facing modification**: Once a parent has accepted a credit arrangement, neither the parent nor the bursar can modify the credit amount or repayment schedule within Zeraki Finance. Modifications require coordination with the lending partner outside of this system.
- **BR-13 — School dashboard is read-only**: The bursar can view credit arrangement data but cannot approve, decline, modify, or cancel arrangements. The credit relationship is between the parent and the lending partner; Zeraki is the data and routing layer.
- **BR-14 — Offer language**: The word "loan" must not appear in any parent-facing communication in entry flows (offer SMS, offer screen, consent step 1 initiation). The consent disclosure steps may use precise legal language as required by the lending partner or CBK. After acceptance, repayment communications may use neutral language ("fee top-up balance").
- **BR-15 — Repayment period**: The repayment period for a credit arrangement is 4–8 weeks from the date of disbursement. The exact period for each arrangement is calculated at acceptance time based on the credit amount and the parent's estimated payment cadence. It must not extend beyond the end of the current term.
- **BR-16 — Repayment frequency**: Repayment is weekly. One repayment instalment is due per week. The waterfall deducts the weekly repayment share from the next fee payment received after each weekly interval.
- **BR-17 — Credit fee**: The target cost to the parent is 5% flat of the disbursed amount, applied once at origination (not monthly, not compounding). This is a negotiation target for the lending partner agreement. If the partner requires a higher rate, the maximum acceptable rate for this product is [to be confirmed with lending partner — see OQ-01]. The flat fee must be displayed to the parent in the consent flow as a total cost in KES (e.g., *"Total repayment: KES [credit_amount + flat_fee]"*), not as a percentage.
- **BR-18 — No rollover**: If the full credit balance has not been repaid by the end of the current term's waterfall window (4–8 weeks from disbursement), the outstanding balance does not automatically roll over to the next term. The lending partner is notified of the remaining balance. The resolution path (extended collection, CRB listing, or write-off) is determined by the lending partner — not by Zeraki Finance.
- **BR-19 — No compounding**: Interest does not compound. The flat fee is applied once at disbursement. The total repayment amount is fixed at `credit_amount + flat_fee` and does not increase over time, regardless of how long repayment takes within the waterfall window.
- **BR-20 — STOP must be session-scoped**: A parent who replies STOP during a credit consent SMS session (Flow 5) must have only that consent session terminated. STOP must not trigger a global SMS opt-out from the school's sender ID. A global opt-out would silently remove the parent from Layer 1 instalment reminders, fee notifications, and all other school SMS communications — a product-breaking side effect that the parent did not intend and cannot easily reverse. The technical implementation must intercept STOP at the application layer before it reaches any carrier-level opt-out processing. This must be confirmed with the SMS provider before this feature ships.

## Out of Scope

- General-purpose lending to parents for non-fee purposes
- Zeraki holding a loan book or lending licence
- Active debt recovery — Zeraki does not chase defaulting parents; that is the lending partner's function
- CRB listing operations — the lending partner handles CRB reporting; Zeraki's role ends at notifying the partner when the waterfall breaks
- Credit arrangements for schools (school-level lending is a separate product entirely)
- Layer 2 in markets without M-Pesa paybill infrastructure — requires separate architecture per market, out of Phase 1 scope
- Parent-facing credit history or repayment statement view beyond the SMS notifications defined in this spec
