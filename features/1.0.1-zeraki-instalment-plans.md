# Feature: Zeraki Instalment Plans

## Overview
**What it does**: Turns a Zeraki Finance pledge record into a structured instalment plan — parents can request a plan via the Zeraki app or SMS, or the bursar creates one after an in-person conversation. The school configures plan templates to keep the bursar's workload manageable. Bursars approve, adjust, or decline requests via a queue. The system tracks per-instalment adherence with two metrics — on-time rate and completion rate — and sends automated SMS notifications at every meaningful event.
**Why it exists**: The existing Zeraki Finance pledge feature is a digital notebook entry with no workflow, no parent agency, and no consequence. The dignity argument is central: parents should be able to request a plan without a gate negotiation. This feature gives the pledge meaning — a real plan both parties can track — and generates the adherence data that Layer 2 (fee top-up credit) requires.
**Phase**: Phase 1
**Depends on**: Existing Zeraki Finance school subscription, student enrolment records, fee balance records, and M-Pesa paybill integration (for payment ingestion)
**This is NOT**:
- A credit or lending product — no money is disbursed, no interest is charged
- A collections enforcement tool — the system tracks and alerts; it does not send children home or impose penalties
- A replacement for the existing fee receipting flow — payments are still made to the school's paybill as normal; the plan layer sits on top
- A fully autonomous system — the bursar retains approval authority unless the school enables auto-approve

---

## Actors

- **Headteacher / School Admin**: Opts the school into the instalment plans feature. Configures plan templates and approval settings (manual approval queue vs. auto-approve).
- **Bursar**: Processes the approval queue (Approve / Adjust / Decline). Creates custom plans for in-person conversations. Monitors adherence and plan status via the dashboard.
- **Parent**: Requests a plan via the Zeraki app (Path A) or SMS keyword (Path B). Receives SMS notifications at every plan event. Makes payments to the school's existing paybill as normal.

---

## Preconditions

- The school has an active Zeraki Finance subscription
- The Headteacher or School Admin has enabled instalment plans via Settings (Flow 1 must have run)
- At least one plan template is configured for the current term (required for Path A and Path B)
- The student has an outstanding fee balance for the current term
- The parent has a phone number registered against the student record in Zeraki Finance
- The bursar is authenticated as a Zeraki Finance user with fee management permissions

---

## Core Flows

### Flow 1: School Enables Instalment Plans and Configures Settings

**Trigger**: Headteacher or designated admin navigates to Settings → Fee Collection → Instalment Plans.

**Steps**:
1. Admin opens the Instalment Plans settings screen.
2. System displays the feature description and the school commitment statement: *"By enabling instalment plans, this school agrees that any student with an active plan that is being honoured will not be sent home for fee arrears during the plan period."*
3. Admin confirms the commitment by clicking **Enable Instalment Plans**.
4. System activates the feature and prompts the admin to configure plan templates for the current term (required before parents can request plans via app or SMS).
5. Admin creates up to 3 plan templates (see Template Data Contract). Each template defines a name and instalment schedule structure. Example: *"Weekly: 4 payments"*, *"Fortnightly: 2 payments"*, *"Single lump sum: 1 payment"*.
6. Admin selects the approval mode:
   - **Manual approval** (default): all parent plan requests enter the bursar's approval queue
   - **Auto-approve**: parent plan requests matching a template are automatically approved without bursar review
7. Admin saves settings.
8. System confirms: *"Instalment plans are now enabled. Parents can request plans via the Zeraki app or by SMS. Bursars can create plans directly from the student fee screen."*

**Outcome**: The school is opted in. Templates are configured. Approval mode is set. Plan requests from parents become available.

**Error — Admin tries to enable without creating at least one template**:
- System blocks save and displays: *"Add at least one plan template before enabling instalment plans. Templates let parents request a plan without calling the school."*

**Error — Admin tries to create more than 3 templates**:
- The **Add Template** button is disabled once 3 templates exist. System shows: *"You have reached the maximum of 3 templates per term."*

---

### Flow 2: Parent Requests a Plan via the Zeraki App (Path A)

**Trigger**: Parent opens the Zeraki app, navigates to their child's fee screen, and selects **Request a Payment Plan**.

**Steps**:
1. Parent selects **Request a Payment Plan** on their child's current term fee screen.
2. System displays the outstanding fee balance and the school's available plan templates.
3. Parent selects a template.
4. System calculates and displays the instalment schedule: due dates (based on today's date and the template interval) and instalment amounts (outstanding balance divided equally across instalments, rounded to the nearest whole KES — any rounding remainder added to the first instalment).
5. Parent reviews the proposed schedule and confirms.
6. System creates a plan request in **PENDING_APPROVAL** status.
7. **If school is set to auto-approve**: system immediately approves the request, creates an ACTIVE plan, and skips to step 8.
8. System sends a confirmation SMS to the parent: *"Your payment plan request for [Student name] has been received and is being reviewed. You'll get an update shortly."* (Auto-approve variant: *"Your payment plan for [Student name] has been set up. [Summary of schedule]. Pay to [paybill] as usual."*)
9. Bursar's approval queue shows the new request (unless auto-approved).

**Outcome**: A plan request exists. Parent receives SMS confirmation. Bursar sees the request in the queue (or plan is immediately ACTIVE if auto-approved).

**Error — No templates configured for this term**:
- System displays: *"Payment plans are not available yet this term. Please contact the school directly."*
- No request is created.

**Error — Student already has an active plan this term**:
- System displays: *"[Student name] already has an active payment plan this term. Contact the school if you need to make changes."*
- No new request is created.

---

### Flow 3: Parent Requests a Plan via SMS (Path B)

**Trigger**: Parent sends the keyword **PLAN** to the school's inbound SMS number.

**Steps**:
1. Parent sends "PLAN" to the school's SMS number.
2. System identifies the parent from the registered phone number. If multiple students are linked to this number, system asks the parent to specify: *"Reply with the student number: 1. [Student A] 2. [Student B]"*
3. System sends the available templates as an SMS: *"[School name] payment plans for [Student name]: Reply 1 for [Template 1 name] | Reply 2 for [Template 2 name] | Reply 3 for [Template 3 name]"*
4. Parent replies with a number (1, 2, or 3).
5. System sends the calculated schedule: *"Plan: [N] payments of KES [amount]. Due dates: [date 1], [date 2]... Reply YES to confirm or NO to cancel."*
6. Parent replies **YES**.
7. System creates a plan request in **PENDING_APPROVAL** status (or immediately ACTIVE if auto-approve is enabled).
8. System sends confirmation SMS (same content as Path A step 8).
9. Bursar's approval queue shows the new request (unless auto-approved).

**Outcome**: A plan request exists via SMS. Identical outcome to Path A.

**Error — Phone number not found in Zeraki**:
- System replies: *"We couldn't find your details. Please contact [School name] directly."*
- No request is created.

**Error — Parent replies with an invalid option**:
- System replies: *"Please reply with 1, 2, or 3 to choose a plan, or contact the school directly."*
- SMS session remains open for 10 minutes awaiting a valid reply. After 10 minutes, session expires silently.

**Error — Parent replies NO at confirmation step**:
- System replies: *"No plan has been set up. You can start over by sending PLAN again."*
- No request is created.

---

### Flow 4: Bursar Creates a Plan Directly (Path C — In-Person)

**Trigger**: Bursar opens a student's fee record and selects **Create Plan**.

**Steps**:
1. Bursar navigates to the student's fee record for the current term.
2. Bursar selects **Create Plan**.
3. System displays the outstanding fee balance and presents two options:
   - **Use a template** — shows the school's configured templates with pre-calculated schedules
   - **Custom plan** — bursar enters plan amount, number of instalments, and specific due dates manually
4. Bursar selects a template or enters a custom plan.
5. System validates (same rules as parent-initiated request — see Business Rules).
6. Bursar reviews and confirms.
7. System creates the plan directly in **ACTIVE** status (no approval queue — the bursar is the approver).
8. System sends a plan confirmation SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Plan Active).

**Outcome**: An ACTIVE plan exists immediately. No approval queue step. Parent is notified via SMS.

**Error — Plan already exists for this student this term**:
- System blocks creation: *"[Student name] already has an active plan this term. View or cancel the existing plan before creating a new one."*

---

### Flow 5: Bursar Processes the Approval Queue

**Trigger**: Bursar opens the Instalment Plans section and sees pending plan requests.

**Steps**:
1. Bursar opens the approval queue. Each request shows: student name, parent name, requested template, calculated schedule, outstanding balance.
2. For each request, the bursar selects one of three actions:

   **Approve**:
   - Bursar clicks **Approve**.
   - System transitions the request to APPROVED and creates an ACTIVE plan matching the requested template and schedule.
   - System sends the parent a plan active SMS (see SMS Contracts — Plan Active).

   **Adjust**:
   - Bursar clicks **Adjust**.
   - System opens the plan editor pre-filled with the parent's requested schedule.
   - Bursar modifies instalment amounts, due dates, or count as needed.
   - Bursar saves and approves the adjusted plan.
   - System transitions the request to APPROVED and creates an ACTIVE plan with the bursar's adjusted schedule.
   - System sends the parent a plan active SMS noting any adjustment: *"Your payment plan for [Student name] has been set up with a small adjustment. [Summary]. Pay to [paybill] as usual."*

   **Decline**:
   - Bursar clicks **Decline** and optionally enters a reason.
   - System transitions the request to DECLINED.
   - System sends the parent a decline SMS: *"Your payment plan request for [Student name] could not be approved. Please contact [School name] to discuss your options."* (If bursar entered a reason: *"...could not be approved: [reason]. Please contact..."*)

3. **Approve All**: Bursar clicks **Approve All** to bulk-approve all currently pending requests without modification. System approves each and sends plan active SMS to each parent.

**Outcome**: Each request is either APPROVED (plan becomes ACTIVE), DECLINED, or ADJUSTED-then-APPROVED.

---

### Flow 6: Automated SMS Reminder Sent to Parent Before Due Date

**Trigger**: System scheduled job runs daily and checks for instalments due within the reminder window.

**Steps**:
1. System identifies all PENDING instalments whose due date falls within the next [N] days (see Open Questions — OQ-01).
2. For each identified instalment, system checks:
   - Instalment is still PENDING (not already paid)
   - Plan is still ACTIVE
   - A reminder SMS has not already been sent for this instalment in this reminder cycle
3. System sends reminder SMS to the parent's registered phone number from the school's sender ID.
4. System records the SMS as sent (timestamp, delivery status) against the instalment record.

**Outcome**: Parent receives reminder SMS. Bursar dashboard shows reminder sent status per instalment.

**SMS delivery failure**:
- System marks delivery status FAILED on the instalment record.
- Bursar dashboard surfaces the failure.
- Bursar can manually resend from the instalment detail screen.

---

### Flow 7: Payment Matched to Active Plan

**Trigger**: A fee payment is received on the school's M-Pesa paybill for a student who has an ACTIVE instalment plan.

**Steps**:
1. Payment event arrives via the existing Zeraki Finance paybill webhook.
2. System checks whether the paying student has an ACTIVE plan this term.
3. System allocates the payment against the oldest PENDING instalment first (FIFO).
   - If payment ≥ instalment amount: instalment is marked **PAID_ON_TIME** (paid on or before due date) or **PAID_LATE** (paid after due date). Surplus applied to next PENDING instalment, then to general fee balance.
   - If payment < instalment amount: payment recorded as partial. Instalment remains PENDING until full amount received.
4. System recalculates both adherence metrics (see Business Rules — BR-10 and BR-11).
5. System sends payment received SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Payment Received).
6. System updates the bursar dashboard.
7. If all instalments are now PAID_ON_TIME or PAID_LATE: system transitions the plan to **COMPLETED** and sends plan completion SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Plan Complete).

**Outcome**: Payment recorded, adherence updated, parent notified.

**Edge — Payment received after instalment due date but before plan closes**:
- Instalment is marked PAID_LATE. Completion rate is incremented. On-time rate is not.

**Edge — Payment exceeds total plan amount**:
- All remaining instalments are marked PAID_ON_TIME. Plan transitions to COMPLETED. Surplus credited to general fee balance.

---

### Flow 8: Bursar Records Manual Payment Against Plan (Cash or Bank Deposit)

**Trigger**: A parent pays school fees in cash or via bank deposit. The bursar receipts the payment in Zeraki Finance and applies it against the student's active plan.

**Steps**:
1. Bursar opens the student's fee record and selects **Record Payment**.
2. System displays the payment entry form:
   - **Amount** (KES, required)
   - **Date** (required — defaults to today, can be backdated to the actual payment date)
   - **Payment method**: Cash / Bank deposit
   - **Reference number** (optional — e.g., bank deposit slip number, cash receipt number)
3. Bursar submits the form.
4. **Duplicate detection — before applying**: system checks whether a payment record already exists for this student with the same amount (exact match) on the same calendar date or ±1 calendar day, entered via a different channel (M-Pesa auto-match or a separate manual entry). See Duplicate Flag rules below.
5. **If no duplicate detected**: system applies the payment to the active plan using the same FIFO allocation logic as Flow 7:
   - Allocated against the oldest PENDING instalment first
   - Instalment marked PAID_ON_TIME (if entered date ≤ due date) or PAID_LATE (if entered date > due date)
   - Surplus applied to next PENDING instalment, then to general fee balance
   - Both adherence metrics recalculated
6. System receipts the payment in Zeraki Finance (standard receipting behaviour — no change to existing flow).
7. System sends a payment received SMS to the parent (same as Flow 7 — see SMS Contracts — Payment Received).
8. Bursar dashboard updated.

**Duplicate Flag — manual entry triggers check**:
System surfaces a resolution prompt before applying the payment:
*"A [M-Pesa / manual] payment of KES [amount] was already recorded for [Student name] on [date]. Is this the same payment or a separate one?"*
- **Same payment — dismiss this entry**: the new manual entry is discarded. No allocation occurs. No double-counting.
- **Separate payment — apply both**: both payments are applied independently. The bursar accepts responsibility for the resolution decision. System logs the override.

**Duplicate Flag — M-Pesa auto-match triggers check (reverse case)**:
When a paybill webhook payment arrives (Flow 7) and a manual entry for the same student, same amount, same date ±1 calendar day already exists:
- The webhook payment is held in **PENDING_REVIEW** status. It is not auto-applied.
- The bursar dashboard shows a duplicate flag: *"[Student name]: a KES [amount] M-Pesa payment may duplicate a manual entry from [date]. Review before applying."*
- Bursar resolves via the same two options (dismiss webhook / apply both).
- Until resolved, neither payment affects the adherence metrics.

**Outcome**: Manual payment is applied to the plan using identical logic to M-Pesa auto-match. Cash-paying parents are correctly reflected as paid in the adherence record. Duplicate risk is surfaced before it corrupts plan data.

**Error — Amount entered exceeds the plan's remaining balance**:
- System applies the payment up to the plan total. Surplus credited to the student's general fee balance. Matches surplus handling in Flow 7.

**Error — No active plan exists for this student this term**:
- System receipts the payment as a standard fee payment (existing Zeraki Finance behaviour). No plan matching occurs. No SMS is sent by this feature.

---

### Flow 9: Instalment Marked MISSED — Parent Notified

**Trigger**: System scheduled job runs daily and checks for overdue instalments.

**Steps**:
1. System identifies all PENDING instalments whose due date has passed by more than [grace period] days (see OQ-02).
2. For each: if no full payment received, system marks the instalment **MISSED**.
3. System recalculates both adherence metrics.
4. System sends an overdue SMS to the **parent** (see SMS Contracts — Overdue Notice).
5. System sends an alert to the **bursar** (see SMS Contracts — Bursar Missed Alert).
6. If all remaining instalments are MISSED and the term has ended: system transitions the plan to **DEFAULTED**.

**Outcome**: Parent and bursar are both notified. Adherence metrics are updated. The plan remains ACTIVE unless all instalments default.

---

### Flow 10: Parent Checks Fee Balance via SMS (BAL Keyword)

**Trigger**: Parent sends the keyword **BAL** to the school's inbound SMS number.

**Steps**:
1. Parent sends "BAL" to the school's SMS number.
2. System identifies the parent from the registered phone number. If multiple students, system returns a summary for each.
3. System sends balance SMS: *"[Student name] fees: Balance due KES [amount]. Active plan: [Y/N]. [If yes]: Next instalment KES [amount] due [date]."*

**Outcome**: Parent receives their current fee balance and plan status without calling the school.

**Error — Phone number not found**:
- System replies: *"We couldn't find your details. Please contact [School name] directly."*

---

### Flow 11: Bursar Views the Instalment Plan Dashboard

**Trigger**: Bursar navigates to the Instalment Plans dashboard.

**Steps**:
1. Bursar opens the Instalment Plans section in Zeraki Finance.
2. System displays the term-level summary:
   - Total active plans this term
   - Total KES expected from plans this term
   - Total KES received against plans this term
   - Number of plans fully on track (no missed instalments, all paid on time)
   - Number of plans with at least one PAID_LATE instalment
   - Number of plans with at least one MISSED instalment
   - Pending approval queue count (with one-click access to the queue)
   - Upcoming instalments due in the next 7 days (student name, amount, due date)
   - Overdue instalments (student name, amount, days overdue)
3. Bursar can drill into an individual student's plan to see the full instalment schedule, payment history, and both adherence metrics.

**Outcome**: Bursar has a live view of all active plans, the approval queue, upcoming obligations, and payment gaps.

---

### Flow 12: Bursar Cancels a Plan

**Trigger**: Bursar selects **Cancel Plan** on an active student plan.

**Steps**:
1. Bursar opens the student's active plan and selects **Cancel Plan**.
2. System prompts: *"Cancelling this plan will remove all remaining scheduled instalments. Payments already received will remain recorded. Are you sure?"*
3. Bursar confirms.
4. System transitions the plan to **CANCELLED**. All PENDING instalments are marked CANCELLED.
5. System sends a cancellation SMS to the parent (see SMS Contracts — Plan Cancelled).
6. System records who cancelled and the timestamp.

**Outcome**: Plan is cancelled. All adherence data for completed instalments is preserved and immutable.

## Data Contracts

### Plan Template (configured by school admin)
```json
{
  "template_id": "uuid",
  "school_id": "uuid",
  "term_id": "uuid",
  "name": "string | required | max: 60 characters",
  "instalment_count": "integer | required | min: 1 | max: 12",
  "interval_type": "weekly | fortnightly | monthly | custom",
  "is_active": "boolean | default: true",
  "created_at": "ISO8601 datetime"
}
```
**Note**: Templates define structure only (count + interval). Instalment amounts are calculated at request time by dividing the parent's outstanding balance equally across instalments. Any rounding remainder (due to KES integer rounding) is added to the first instalment.

**Constraint**: Maximum 3 active templates per school per term.

- `plan_amount`: the student's outstanding fee balance at time of request

## SMS Contracts

All SMS messages are sent from the school's registered sender ID. Maximum length: 160 characters per message. Multi-part SMS is allowed for longer messages.

| Event | Recipient | Message |
|---|---|---|
| **Plan request received** (manual approval) | Parent | `[School]: Your plan request for [Student] is being reviewed. You'll hear back shortly.` |
| **Plan active** (approved or auto-approved) | Parent | `[School]: Payment plan set up for [Student]. [N] payments of KES [amount]. Next due: [date]. Pay to [paybill] as usual.` |
| **Plan active — adjusted** | Parent | `[School]: Your plan for [Student] has been set up with a small adjustment. Next due: KES [amount] on [date]. Pay to [paybill] as usual.` |
| **Plan declined** | Parent | `[School]: Your plan request for [Student] could not be approved. [Reason if provided.] Please contact the school.` |
| **Instalment reminder** | Parent | `[School]: Reminder — KES [amount] due for [Student] on [date]. Pay to [paybill] as usual.` |
| **Payment received** | Parent | `[School]: KES [amount] received for [Student]. Remaining plan balance: KES [amount]. Next due: KES [amount] on [date].` |
| **Plan complete** | Parent | `[School]: [Student]'s payment plan is complete. All instalments paid. Thank you.` |
| **Overdue notice** | Parent | `[School]: KES [amount] for [Student] was due on [date] and has not been received. Please pay to [paybill] or contact the school.` |
| **Missed instalment alert** | Bursar (registered number) | `[Student name]: KES [amount] instalment due [date] has not been paid. Review in Zeraki Finance.` |

| **BAL response** | Parent | `[Student]: Fees due KES [amount]. Plan active: [Yes/No]. [If yes: Next instalment KES [amount] due [date].]` |

## State Machines

### Plan Request Status
```
[PENDING_APPROVAL] --bursar approves--> [APPROVED] --> plan created as ACTIVE
[PENDING_APPROVAL] --bursar adjusts and approves--> [ADJUSTED] --> plan created as ACTIVE
[PENDING_APPROVAL] --bursar declines--> [DECLINED]
[PENDING_APPROVAL] --auto-approve enabled--> [APPROVED] --> plan created as ACTIVE (immediate)
```

### Plan Status
```
[ACTIVE] --all instalments PAID_ON_TIME or PAID_LATE--> [COMPLETED]
[ACTIVE] --all remaining instalments MISSED, term ended--> [DEFAULTED]
[ACTIVE] --bursar cancels--> [CANCELLED]
```

### Instalment Status
```
[PENDING] --full payment received on or before due_date--> [PAID_ON_TIME]
[PENDING] --full payment received after due_date--> [PAID_LATE]
[PENDING] --due_date + grace period passes, no full payment--> [MISSED]
[PENDING] --plan cancelled--> [CANCELLED]
```
**Note**: Partial payments do not change instalment status. They reduce the remaining amount owed. The instalment transitions only when the full amount is received.

## Business Rules

- **BR-01**: A student may have at most one ACTIVE instalment plan per term. Attempting to create or request a second plan returns `PLAN_ALREADY_EXISTS`.
- **BR-02**: The sum of all instalment amounts must equal the plan amount exactly at creation time. No tolerance. For template-derived plans, any KES rounding remainder is added to the first instalment.
- **BR-03**: All instalment due dates must fall within the current term window (term start ≤ due date ≤ term end date).
- **BR-04**: Instalment due dates must be in strictly ascending order. No two instalments may share the same due date.
- **BR-05**: Payments are allocated to the oldest PENDING instalment first (FIFO). Surplus beyond the plan total is credited to the general fee balance.
- **BR-06**: An instalment is PAID_ON_TIME if the full instalment amount is received on or before the due date. It is PAID_LATE if received after the due date.
- **BR-07**: An instalment is marked MISSED if the full amount has not been received within [grace period] days after the due date. (See OQ-02.)
- **BR-08**: A school may configure a maximum of 3 active plan templates per term. Templates can be deactivated but not deleted once at least one plan has used them.
- **BR-09**: Auto-approve applies only to parent-initiated requests that match a configured template. Bursar-created plans (Path C) are never subject to the approval queue — they are created as ACTIVE immediately.
- **BR-10 — On-Time Rate**: `on_time_rate = count(PAID_ON_TIME) / count(instalments where due_date has passed and status ≠ CANCELLED)`. Range: 0.00–1.00. Null if no instalments have passed their due date.
- **BR-11 — Completion Rate**: `completion_rate = count(PAID_ON_TIME + PAID_LATE) / count(instalments where due_date has passed and status ≠ CANCELLED)`. Range: 0.00–1.00. Null if no instalments have passed their due date.
- **BR-12**: Both metrics are recalculated after every payment allocation event and every MISSED transition. They are stored on the plan record, not computed on read.
- **BR-13**: Cancelling a plan preserves all adherence data for instalments that were already resolved (PAID_ON_TIME, PAID_LATE, MISSED). Only PENDING instalments are marked CANCELLED. Historical records are immutable.
- **BR-14**: SMS messages are sent from the school's registered sender ID. Parents see the school's name in their SMS inbox, not Zeraki's.
- **BR-15**: The BAL keyword response includes plan status only if an ACTIVE plan exists for the current term. It does not expose historical plan data.
- **BR-16**: The school opt-in commitment ("students on an active, honoured plan will not be sent home") is a displayed agreement at settings activation. Zeraki Finance does not technically enforce this. A plan is considered "honoured" if no instalment is currently in MISSED status.
- **BR-17**: Manual cash and bank deposit payments use identical FIFO allocation logic to M-Pesa auto-matched payments (Flow 7). The payment method does not affect how payments are applied to plan milestones. On-time vs. late status is determined by the bursar-entered payment date, not the entry date in the system.
- **BR-18 — Duplicate detection trigger**: A duplicate flag is raised when a payment for the same student, same exact amount (in KES cents), on the same calendar date or ±1 calendar day is detected across two different payment records (M-Pesa vs. manual, or manual vs. manual). The flag must be resolved by the bursar before either payment is applied to the plan or affects adherence metrics.
- **BR-19 — Pending duplicate holds**: A webhook payment held in PENDING_REVIEW status (awaiting duplicate resolution) does not affect the instalment's status, the plan's adherence metrics, or the parent's SMS notifications until the bursar resolves the flag. If the bursar resolves as "apply both," the payments are applied in chronological order (earlier date first).

## Out of Scope

- Parent self-service plan modification after approval — parents request; bursars approve and adjust
- Credit scoring, lending, or any disbursement — covered by `1.0.2-zeraki-fee-top-up-credit.md`
- Enforcement of the "child stays in school" commitment — Zeraki surfaces plan status; school-side compliance is behavioural
- Integration with external credit bureaus — Layer 2 concern
- Multi-school plan visibility — each plan is scoped to a single school and term
- Fee waiver or discount workflows — waivers are a separate fee management concern
- WhatsApp or USSD channel for parent requests — SMS and app only for MVP
