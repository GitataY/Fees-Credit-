# Feature 1 — School Enables Fee Top-Up Credit

Feature: School enables Fee Top-Up Credit
  As a headteacher or school admin
  I want to opt my school into Fee Top-Up Credit
  So that eligible parents can access affordable fee support through Zeraki

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Admin enables Fee Top-Up Credit after Layer 1 is already active
    Given the school has an active Zeraki Finance subscription
    And instalment plans (Layer 1) are already enabled for the school
    And the admin is authenticated with settings permissions
    When the admin navigates to Settings → Fee Collection → Fee Top-Up Credit
    And the admin reviews the product summary and school commitment
    And the admin clicks Enable Fee Top-Up Credit
    Then Fee Top-Up Credit is activated for the school
    And the admin sees: "Fee Top-Up Credit is now enabled. Eligible parents will be identified at the start of each term and offered fee support."
    And the school is queued for the next eligibility assessment batch

  # ---- Error Paths ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @error-path
  Scenario: Admin tries to enable Fee Top-Up Credit before Layer 1 is enabled
    Given instalment plans (Layer 1) have not been enabled for the school
    When the admin attempts to enable Fee Top-Up Credit
    Then the system blocks activation
    And the admin sees: "Please enable Instalment Plans first. Fee Top-Up Credit requires instalment plan data to assess eligibility for some parents."

# Feature 2 — Eligibility Assessment

Feature: System runs the eligibility assessment batch for Fee Top-Up Credit
  As the Zeraki system
  I want to identify credit-eligible parents at opted-in schools
  So that offers are only sent to parents with a qualifying payment track record

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And the student has an outstanding fee balance for the current term
    And the parent has a phone number registered against the student in Zeraki Finance

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Path 1 eligible parent receives an eligibility record with a credit tier and offer amount
    Given the parent has at least 4 terms of fee payment history in Zeraki Finance
    And the parent's average payment speed score across the last 4 terms is 90 or above
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs
    Then the system calculates the parent's weighted credit score across all five components
    And maps the score to a credit tier (A, B, or C)
    And calculates the maximum credit offer amount based on the tier formula and the student's term fee
    And creates an eligibility record for the parent with Path 1 as the primary eligibility signal
    And sends the eligibility record to the lending partner for pre-approval

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Path 2 eligible parent with a strong completed instalment plan receives an eligibility record
    Given the parent has at least one completed Layer 1 instalment plan for this student
    And the most recently completed plan has an on-time rate of 0.75 or above
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs
    Then the system creates an eligibility record for the parent with Path 2 as the primary eligibility signal
    And sends the eligibility record to the lending partner for pre-approval

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Parent qualifying via both paths is recorded with Path 1 as the primary signal
    Given the parent meets both the Path 1 payment speed threshold and the Path 2 on-time rate threshold
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs
    Then the eligibility record is created with Path 1 as the primary eligibility signal
    And both paths are noted on the record
    And the credit offer amount is calculated using the same tier formula regardless of path

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Calculated offer amount below KES 3,000 floor — no offer is created
    Given the parent clears the eligibility threshold for at least one path
    And the parent's credit tier calculation produces a maximum offer amount of KES 2,500
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs
    Then no eligibility record is created for the parent
    And no offer is sent

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Lending partner approves a lower amount than Zeraki's tier maximum
    Given the parent's eligibility record has a Zeraki tier maximum of KES 6,000
    When the lending partner responds with an approved amount of KES 4,500
    Then the eligibility record stores both the Zeraki maximum and the partner-approved amount
    And the offer presented to the parent uses KES 4,500 — not KES 6,000

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Parent with an existing active arrangement this term is excluded from the assessment
    Given the parent already has an active credit arrangement for this student this term
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs
    Then the parent is excluded from offer delivery
    And no second eligibility record is created

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Parent qualifies under Path 1 even when the current term's payment activity is poor
    Given the parent has 6 terms of strong payment history meeting the Path 1 threshold
    And the parent's current term fee payment activity is below the Path 1 threshold
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs mid-term
    Then the current term is excluded from the Path 1 score calculation
    And the parent is still assessed as Path 1 eligible based on their historical track record

  # ---- Error Paths ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @error-path
  Scenario: Parent with fewer than 4 terms of history and no completed Layer 1 plan is not eligible
    Given the parent has 3 terms of fee payment history in Zeraki Finance
    And the parent has no completed Layer 1 instalment plan
    When the eligibility assessment batch runs
    Then no eligibility record is created for the parent
    And no offer is sent

    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled

# Feature 4 — Parent Accepts Offer via App (Two-Step Consent)

Feature: Parent accepts a credit offer via the Zeraki app with two-step consent
  As a parent
  I want to accept a fee top-up offer through the app
  So that fee support is credited to the school on my behalf with my full informed consent

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And the parent has a credit arrangement in OFFERED status
    And the parent is authenticated in the Zeraki app

    And the credit arrangement remains in OFFERED status
    And the parent can return to the offer before it expires

# Feature 5 — Parent Accepts Offer via SMS (Two-Step Consent)

Feature: Parent accepts a credit offer via SMS with two-step consent
  As a parent
  I want to accept a fee top-up offer by SMS
  So that I can accept fee support without needing the app

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And the parent has a credit arrangement in OFFERED status
    And the parent's phone number is registered against the student in Zeraki Finance

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Parent completes two-step SMS consent and arrangement is accepted
    When the parent replies "FEES" to the school's SMS number
    Then the system sends the Consent Step 1 disclosure: "[School] fee top-up: [Student]'s payment history will be shared with our credit partner. Repayments reported to credit bureaus. Reply YES to continue or NO to cancel."
    When the parent replies "YES"
    Then the system sends the Consent Step 2 confirmation: "Confirm: KES [amount] fee top-up for [Student]. ~KES [amount]/[week or month] from your future fee payments. Reply CONFIRM to accept."
    When the parent replies "CONFIRM"
    Then the consent is recorded with the current consent version, timestamp, and channel "sms"
    And the credit arrangement transitions to ACCEPTED status
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: KES [amount] fee top-up confirmed for [Student] and credited to the school. Repayment starts from your next fee payment. Pay to [paybill] as usual."

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Parent replies with something other than YES at the Step 1 confirmation — session stays open
    Given the parent has received the Consent Step 1 disclosure SMS
    When the parent replies with a message other than "YES"
    Then the system replies: "Reply YES to continue or ignore this message to decline."
    And the SMS session remains open for 30 minutes awaiting a valid reply
    And the credit arrangement remains in OFFERED status

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Parent replies with something other than CONFIRM at Step 2 — session stays open
    Given the parent has received the Consent Step 2 confirmation SMS
    When the parent replies with a message other than "CONFIRM"
    Then the system replies: "Reply CONFIRM to complete your fee top-up, or ignore to cancel."
    And the SMS session remains open for 30 minutes
    And the credit arrangement remains in OFFERED status

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: SMS session expires after 30 minutes without a response
    Given the parent has received the Consent Step 1 disclosure SMS
    When 30 minutes pass without the parent sending a valid reply
    Then the SMS session expires
    And the credit arrangement remains in OFFERED status until the offer expiry date
    And the parent can re-initiate by sending the keyword again

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Parent replies STOP during a consent session — only the session is terminated, not all school SMS
    Given the parent has received the Consent Step 1 disclosure SMS
    When the parent replies "STOP"
    Then only the active consent SMS session is terminated
    And the credit arrangement remains in OFFERED status
    And the parent continues to receive Layer 1 instalment reminders and other school SMS communications

# Feature 6 — Parent Declines Offer or Offer Expires

Feature: Parent declines a credit offer or the offer expires without a response
  As a parent
  I want to be able to decline a fee top-up offer
  So that I am not enrolled in anything I did not choose

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And the parent has a credit arrangement in OFFERED status

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  Scenario: Parent actively declines via the app — arrangement is closed with no SMS sent
    When the parent taps "No thanks" on the offer screen in the Zeraki app

    And the decline timestamp and channel are recorded
    And no SMS is sent to the parent

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Parent declines by replying NO or STOP to the offer SMS — no SMS sent
    When the parent replies "NO" to the credit offer SMS
    Then the credit arrangement transitions to DECLINED status

    And no SMS is sent to the parent

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Offer expires without a response — parent receives an expiry SMS
    Given the offer expiry date has been reached with no response from the parent
    When the system processes expired offers
    Then the credit arrangement transitions to EXPIRED status
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: Your fee top-up offer for [Student] has expired. Eligible parents will be reassessed at the start of next term."

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Declined parent's eligibility record is preserved for future term reassessment
    Given a parent has declined a credit offer this term
    When the eligibility assessment runs at the start of the next term
    Then the parent's prior eligibility record is preserved as historical data
    And the parent may be assessed for a new offer if still eligible next term

# Feature 7 — Zeraki Coordinates Disbursement with Lending Partner

Feature: Zeraki coordinates credit disbursement with the lending partner
  As the Zeraki system
  I want to trigger disbursement to the school's paybill once a parent has consented
  So that the school receives the credit amount and the parent's child stays in school

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And a parent has a credit arrangement in ACCEPTED status

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Lending partner confirms disbursement — arrangement transitions to ACTIVE_REPAYMENT
    When Zeraki sends a disbursement request to the lending partner
    And the disbursement request contains the arrangement ID, credit amount, school paybill number, hashed borrower reference, consent timestamp, consent version, and eligibility score
    And the disbursement request does not contain the parent's name or phone number
    And the lending partner disburses to the school's M-Pesa paybill and sends a confirmation webhook
    Then the credit arrangement transitions through DISBURSED to ACTIVE_REPAYMENT status
    And the disbursement amount, lending partner reference, and disbursement timestamp are recorded
    And the bursar's dashboard shows the new disbursement
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: KES [amount] fee top-up confirmed for [Student] and credited to the school. Repayment starts from your next fee payment. Pay to [paybill] as usual."

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Lending partner API is temporarily unreachable — request is queued for retry
    Given the lending partner API does not respond within the timeout window
    When the disbursement request times out
    Then the request is queued for retry after the configured interval
    And the parent is not notified until disbursement is confirmed
    And the arrangement remains in ACCEPTED status during the retry window

  # ---- Error Paths ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @error-path
  Scenario: Lending partner rejects the disbursement request — arrangement is flagged and parent notified
    When the lending partner returns a rejection response to the disbursement request
    Then the credit arrangement transitions to DISBURSEMENT_FAILED status
    And the rejection reason from the lending partner response is recorded
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: We could not process your fee top-up at this time. Please contact Zeraki Finance support or ask your school's bursar to flag this to Zeraki."
    And no automatic retry is triggered

# Feature 8 — Waterfall Repayment Allocation

Feature: Fee payments are split between credit repayment and school balance via the waterfall
  As a parent
  I want my regular fee payments to automatically repay my fee top-up
  So that I don't need to make a separate repayment — paying fees to the school's paybill as usual is enough

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And a student's parent has a credit arrangement in ACTIVE_REPAYMENT status

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Fee payment larger than the repayment instalment is split between repayment share and school balance
    Given the scheduled repayment instalment amount is KES 800
    And the remaining credit balance is KES 3,200
    When a fee payment of KES 2,500 arrives via the school's M-Pesa paybill
    Then KES 800 is allocated as the repayment share and queued for B2B transfer to the lending partner
    And KES 1,700 is credited to the school's fee balance for the student
    And the arrangement's remaining credit balance is reduced to KES 2,400
    And a waterfall allocation event is recorded
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: KES 2,500 received for [Student]. KES 800 applied to fee top-up. Remaining: KES 2,400. School fees: KES 1,700 credited."

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Payment smaller than the scheduled repayment instalment — full payment goes to repayment share
    Given the scheduled repayment instalment amount is KES 800
    When a fee payment of KES 500 arrives
    Then the entire KES 500 is allocated as the repayment share
    And zero is credited to the school's fee balance for this payment
    And the arrangement's remaining balance is reduced by KES 500
    And the parent receives an SMS confirming the full amount was applied to the fee top-up balance

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Payment exceeds the remaining credit balance — excess credited to school after arrangement closes
    Given the remaining credit balance is KES 600
    When a fee payment of KES 2,000 arrives
    Then KES 600 is allocated as the final repayment share
    And KES 1,400 is credited to the school's fee balance
    And the arrangement transitions to COMPLETED status

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Bursar records a manual cash payment for a student with an ACTIVE_REPAYMENT arrangement — waterfall runs first
    Given the student's parent has an ACTIVE_REPAYMENT credit arrangement
    When the bursar records a cash payment via the manual payment entry flow
    Then the waterfall allocation runs on that payment before any Layer 1 FIFO allocation
    And the repayment share is deducted from the cash payment first
    And the remainder is credited to the school's fee balance and applied to the Layer 1 plan per FIFO rules if a plan is active

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Parent with both an active credit arrangement and an active Layer 1 plan — waterfall takes priority
    Given the student's parent has an ACTIVE_REPAYMENT credit arrangement
    And the student also has an ACTIVE Layer 1 instalment plan
    When a fee payment arrives via the school's M-Pesa paybill
    Then the repayment share for the credit arrangement is deducted first
    And the remaining payment amount is then applied to the Layer 1 plan using FIFO allocation rules

# Feature 9 — Credit Arrangement Completed

Feature: Credit arrangement is completed when the full balance is repaid
  As a parent
  I want to be notified when my fee top-up has been fully repaid
  So that I know my commitment is fulfilled and no further deductions will occur

  Background:

    When a fee payment arrives that covers the remaining credit balance in full
    Then the credit arrangement transitions to COMPLETED status
    And the completion timestamp is recorded
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: Your fee top-up for [Student] is fully repaid. Thank you for keeping your commitment."
    And the lending partner receives the arrangement closure notification

# Feature 10 — Waterfall Breaks: Child No Longer Enrolled

Feature: System handles waterfall breakage when a child is withdrawn or transferred
  As the Zeraki system
  I want to flag credit arrangements at risk when a child leaves the school
  So that the lending partner can begin their own recovery process

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And a student's parent has a credit arrangement in ACTIVE_REPAYMENT status

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Child withdrawal detected — arrangement flagged AT_RISK and lending partner notified
    Given the system detects that the student has been withdrawn or transferred from the school
    When the AT_RISK transition is triggered
    Then the credit arrangement transitions to AT_RISK status
    And the lending partner is notified via API that waterfall collection has stopped for this arrangement
    And the parent receives an SMS: "[School]: Your fee top-up balance for [Student] remains outstanding. The credit partner may contact you about the remaining balance."
    And Zeraki records the at-risk event and takes no further recovery action

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: AT_RISK arrangement is resolved by the lending partner — transitions to COMPLETED or DEFAULTED
    Given a credit arrangement is in AT_RISK status
    When the lending partner notifies Zeraki that the arrangement has been resolved
    Then the arrangement transitions to COMPLETED or DEFAULTED based on the lending partner's resolution

# Feature 11 — Bursar Views the Credit Dashboard

Feature: Bursar monitors credit arrangement activity via the dashboard
  As a bursar
  I want visibility into credit disbursements and repayment status
  So that I understand the financial impact on the school's fee collection this term

  Background:
    Given the school has Fee Top-Up Credit enabled
    And the bursar is authenticated in Zeraki Finance with fee management permissions

  # ---- Happy Path ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Bursar opens the credit dashboard and sees the term-level summary
    Given the school has active and completed credit arrangements for the current term
    When the bursar opens the Fee Top-Up Credit section in Zeraki Finance
    Then the bursar sees the total KES disbursed to the school via credit arrangements this term
    And the count of ACTIVE_REPAYMENT arrangements
    And the count of COMPLETED arrangements this term
    And the count of AT_RISK arrangements if any exist
    And a per-student list showing student name, credit amount, disbursed date, repayment collected to date, remaining balance, and arrangement status

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Bursar views the full timeline for an individual credit arrangement
    When the bursar selects a student from the dashboard
    Then the bursar sees the arrangement's disbursement event, all waterfall allocation events, and the current remaining balance

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @happy-path
  Scenario: Bursar views upcoming repayment instalments on an individual arrangement
    When the bursar opens an individual credit arrangement detail screen
    Then the bursar sees the upcoming repayment instalment schedule
    And each upcoming instalment shows its due date, amount, and status (pending, collected, or missed)

  # ---- Edge Cases ----

  @phase1 @fee-top-up-credit @edge-case
  Scenario: Bursar cannot modify, approve, or cancel a credit arrangement
    When the bursar opens any credit arrangement detail screen
    Then no approve, decline, modify, or cancel actions are available
